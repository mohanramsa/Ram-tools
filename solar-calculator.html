What is a Solar Panel System Calculator?

This free solar calculator helps homeowners, engineers, and solar installers accurately size a solar PV system. Enter your load requirement, peak sun hours, and system voltage — the tool instantly calculates the number of mono solar panels, battery bank size, MPPT charge controller rating, and inverter size required.

📐 Solar Panel Count Formula Daily Energy (kWh) = Load (kW) × Usage Hours
Required Array (kW) = Daily Energy / (Peak Sun Hours × Efficiency)
No. of Panels = Required Array (W) / Panel Wattage — always round up

⚡ System Sizing Note: 12V for loads up to ~1.5kW | 24V for 1.5–3kW | 48V for 3kW+ loads. Higher voltage = less current = thinner cables.

❓ How to Use This Solar Calculator

  1. Select your system voltage (12V, 24V, or 48V) based on your load size
  2. Enter your maximum load in kW (total connected load)
  3. Enter peak sun hours for your location (India: 5–6h, Middle East: 6–7h)
  4. Set system efficiency (typically 75–85% for real-world losses)
  5. Enter daily usage hours (how many hours per day the load runs)
  6. Select panel wattage and click Calculate
